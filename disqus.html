Using the Disqus commenting platform for your Drupal site

26 March 2013

You may decide to use the Disqus service for comments on your Drupal site as an alternative to Drupal's built-in comments. There is a good article about why you might do so here.

If you are starting from scratch for a given Content type, the Drupal settings are straightforward and described on the Disqus project page (link above). If you decide to change commenting from Drupal to Disqus, things are slightly different. You need to change Comment settings for your Content type from Open to Closed. However, nodes that you created before implementing Disqus will still have comments Open. To ensure that new comments on these nodes are set to Disqus, disable Post comments under admin/people/permissions.

There are two ways of dealing with Drupal comments on nodes that are now set to Disqus:

  • You can leave them as they are. Under admin/people/permissions, enable View comments to allow users to view Drupal comments. These will appear under Disqus comments, a bit confusing but less work for the site administrator.
  • You can migrate the comments to Disqus. There is no module in Drupal 7 to do this, but here is a post that tells you how to do it. For a small site, you might even consider transferring the comments manually.

More on Disqus

If you delete anonymous comments, they show up under All on your Disqus Admin page, rather than under Delete, which is a bit confusing.

The default setting for your Disqus comments includes Around the Web Links. To change this, go to Admin -> Settings -> Discovery and change your setting from Maximum increased traffic and revenue to Increased traffic. There's a really good video that explains exactly what to do.
